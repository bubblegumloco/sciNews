Quick Hits

"Spinning Up" Improvements For Quantum Sensors

Source: University of Innsbruck

A squeezed quantum state was produced when spin-exchange interactions were used to entangle 51 ions to an individual particle. Quantum physicists at University of Innsbruck determined that quantum entanglements can improve atomic clocks by increasing the sensitivity of quantum sensors.

New Biomaterial On The Rise

Source: University of New South Wales

Scientists at University of New South Wales developed a hydrogel that both antimicrobial and mimics human tissue. A potential use for this new innovation includes 3D bioprinting to produce material for biomedical research. This can reduce the demand for animal-derived materials.

Brainstorming Ways To Prevent Cyberattacks

Source: University of South Australia

A cyberattack experiment tested an algorithm built by researchers at University of South Australia on a robot operating system(ROS). Deep learning neural networks simulated the behavior of the human brain and ROS learned patterns of the cyberattack. The algorithm was able to stop the cyberattack within seconds and it was 99% successful.
